Tinted Windows

By Raman Baig

A lone flower, stands petrified

As weeds choke it from all surrounding air

It’s roots, grasped by devilish hands

As it cries for help, while the blind watch along

At a horrific scheme

The weak being held captive, is only to them,

A matter of a plant

They say it’s just a plant,

But what their eyes don’t see

Are insects crawling all over the plant,

Feeding on and tearing it to pieces

 

As the weeds swim towards the flower,

It fights the wave with nothing but pricks

Being face to face with martyrdom

It looks down upon them, with no hesitation

With not a wall to put its back against,

Knowing well that it will drown,

The flower breathes its last fumes,

As the reaper comes for its soul,

In front of a global garden

 

With no walking stick to know the right way

Many observe blindly, not a hint of remorse or guilt

Their humanity has evaporated,

Forcing them to walk into rivers, rivers of mud

As for the deafened, they listen along to the cries

And feel relaxed by this burning song

 

The falsely impaired are not the worst,

Unlike the ones who see all, hear all

Whose hearts are covered with a veil of fire

As they drop their hearts into icy water.

The truth is frozen, along with those voices

Voices, that pleaded to be free from their cage

Those hearts truly run this evil show

And in that heartless show, seated is

The falsely disabled, as the flower cries, completely deserted
