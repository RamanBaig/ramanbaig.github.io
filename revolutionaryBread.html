Revolutionary Bread

By Raman Baig

These posters I see,

Speak the same message

These announcements that I hear,

Sing the same song.

A noteless song, filled with mourning,

Of its lover’s absence

And an empty ravine, reeking of hunger

 

The street’s own song is of a disturbing nature

Not a shadow is present, only huge lumps of broken bagpipes.

Yet, I hear a song, filled with notes of terror,

Pulling me into a symphony, free of charge.

Even when I exit the auditorium, I hear a restless roar from within,

For my deflated inner balloon cries for help

 

Shushing my restless animal to sleep,

I remember the last time I fed my lion,

When it looked like a lion’s kid, not of a sloth.

As I remember the past, I excavate a fossil,

And remember two little flowers, flying in the wind,

And a beautiful tree, giving me shade from the heat.

But, they dried up, when the sun stopped beating, and the wind whistling.

 

As I try to bury the fossil away, I peek through the window,

And notice a sweet song, opposing the original

The honourable lady enjoys that revolutionary song

That let the wind speak, and the sun flicker.

And she does so, with a peaceful animal, rivaling mine.

So I gather the people, as the lady enjoys one last time,

And we ready the guillotine, with a freshly redded blade
